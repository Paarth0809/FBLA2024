Pharmaceutical Product Manager

As a Pharmaceutical Product Manager, you must be able to do the following: Conduct Research - You must be able to study current market trends, personas, and competitors. You must also be able to analyze usage data and customer feedback from certain products. This will allow Pharmaceutical Product Managers to know which product to focus on or where to take the next product. Develop Strategies - You must be able to take all your research and compile it all into a high-level strategic plan. Communicate Plans - After you map out strategic plans you must be able to format it into a form that can be communicated across the organization. Coordinate development - You must be able to coordinate multiple teams

Medical Sales Representative

Medical Sales is a very socially oriented job. If you like the sales end of science more than being stuck in an office or lab then this is the job for you. Most of the time you will be talking to hospital personnel, physicians, healthcare executives, and clinics. You will spread information about our products and try selling them to hospitals and clinics. Because of the persuasion needed for this job, you must have great public speaking skills and be very persuasive and confident. You must also be able to keep connections and relationships with customers.

Healthcare Communications Specialist

As a Healthcare communication specialist you must create communication strategies, messages, and products to educate the public, news media, and other audience segments on health issues. This means you must bring light to current health issues and how our company intends on fixing them. Like the Medical Sales Representative role you must have great public speaking skills and be able to create relationships with news networks and local hospitals and clinics.
